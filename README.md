# lab

Public experimentation zone for `qvra`.

`lab` is where unstable prototypes, strange ideas, speculative tools, and sharp unfinished work stay visible long enough to be tested, rejected, promoted, or transformed.

## What belongs here

Artifacts in `lab` should be:
- genuinely experimental
- technically interesting
- directional enough to matter
- early enough that instability is expected
- public enough to create curiosity, argument, or future promotion paths

## What does not belong here

`lab` is not:
- a graveyard
- a place to hide incomplete work forever
- an excuse for weak standards
- a dump of disconnected scraps

## Anchor standard

Anything visible in `lab` should have:
- a clear experiment statement
- a visible reason it exists
- enough structure that others can inspect the direction
- an obvious next fate: promote, split, merge, or kill

## Current role in qvra

`lab` is one of the gravitational anchors.
It keeps the field alive, surprising, and forward-moving.

Related routes:
- [`run`](https://github.com/qvra/run) for tools that have become immediately useful
- [`bench`](https://github.com/qvra/bench) for experiments that deserve real comparison
- [`show`](https://github.com/qvra/show) for experiments that already have visible proof
- [`pulse`](https://github.com/qvra/pulse) for the ongoing motion record of the field
- [`forge`](https://github.com/qvra/forge) for experiments that harden into maintained utility

## Promotion rule

No experiment should stay here by inertia.
It should either move, split, harden, or die.
